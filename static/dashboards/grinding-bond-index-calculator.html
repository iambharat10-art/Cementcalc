What are these factors?
The Bond Work Index is measured in a standard lab ball mill. Real industrial mills are different, so we multiply by conversion factors:
• CCBM (Closed Circuit Ball Mill): The baseline industrial mill. Factor 1.5–2.5× depending on material.
• Roller Press: More efficient than ball mill. CCBM value is divided by 1.8–2.0×.
• VRM (Vertical Roller Mill): Also efficient. CCBM value is divided by 1.7–1.8×.

Complete Guide

What is Bond Work Index?

Imagine you want to know how hard it is to crush a rock. The Bond Work Index (Wi) is a number that tells you exactly that. It's measured in a lab using a small ball mill: you put rock samples in, grind for a set number of rotations, sieve the product, and repeat until a steady state is reached. The result is a number in kWh per ton — how much energy is needed to grind that material from infinitely large to 80% passing 100 µm.

Typical Bond Index values:
• Limestone: 8–14 kWh/t (relatively soft)
• Clinker: 12–18 kWh/t (medium-hard)
• Slag/GGBS: 16–22 kWh/t (hard)
• Quartz: 13–16 kWh/t

From Lab to Real Mill — Why Correction Factors?

The Bond Index is measured under perfect lab conditions. Real mills are different in many ways, so we need 7 correction factors (F1–F7):

FactorWhat It Corrects ForWhen It Applies
F1Wet vs Dry grindingDry grinding needs 30% more energy (F1=1.3)
F2Open vs Closed circuitOpen circuit wastes 3.5% more energy (F2=1.035)
F3Mill diameterLarger mills are more efficient. Fixed at 0.914 for mills ≥3.9m
F4Feed coarsenessVery coarse feed (F80>4000µm) needs extra energy
F5Fine grindingGrinding very fine (P80<70µm) is extra difficult
F6Separator efficiencyHigh-efficiency separators reduce over-grinding
F7Mill type & liningSome linings and designs are more efficient

Converting Between Mill Types

Different grinding machines have very different efficiencies. Once you know the energy for a Closed Circuit Ball Mill (CCBM), you can estimate energy for other mill types by dividing:

• Ball Mill (CCBM): The baseline (×1.0)
• Roller Press: 1.8–2.0× more efficient than ball mill (divide CCBM by 1.8–2.0)
• VRM: 1.7–1.8× more efficient than ball mill (divide CCBM by 1.7–1.8)

Example: If CCBM grindability = 33 kWh/t for clinker:
→ Roller Press = 33 / 1.8 = 18.3 kWh/t
→ VRM = 33 / 1.7 = 19.4 kWh/t

Blaine Fineness vs Power

Blaine measures how fine the powder is (surface area in cm²/g). Finer grinding needs MORE power. The relationship follows a power law:

kWh/t₂ = kWh/t₁ × (Blaine₂ / Blaine₁) ^ n

where n ≈ 1.3–1.5 (the "Blaine exponent")

This means doubling the Blaine roughly doubles the power need (2^1.3 = 2.46×).
Typical Blaine values:
• OPC Cement: 3000–3500 cm²/g
• PPC/Blended: 3200–3800 cm²/g
• GGBS: 3500–4500 cm²/g
• Ultra-fine: 5000–8000 cm²/g

How to Use This Dashboard

Tab 1 — Bond Index → Grindability: Enter your lab Bond Index value and material properties. The calculator applies all 7 correction factors to give you the real-world energy consumption for a CCBM.

Tab 2 — Mill Type Conversion: Compare how much energy each mill type would need for the same job. Enter your Bond Index for limestone or clinker, adjust the conversion factors, and see CCBM, Roller Press, and VRM side by side.

Tab 3 — Blaine vs Power: If you know the power consumption at one Blaine, calculate what it would be at a different Blaine. Also calculates total shaft and motor power for your throughput.
